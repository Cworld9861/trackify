About Pomodoro

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that uses a timer to break work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

  • Focus Time: 25 minutes
  • Short Break: 5 minutes
  • Long Break: 15 minutes (after 4 focus sessions)

About Flowmodoro

The Flowmodoro technique is a flexible time management approach that adapts to your natural workflow. Instead of fixed intervals, you work until your focus begins to wane, then take a proportional break.

  • Flow Time: Work as long as you're focused
  • Break Ratio: 1:5 (5 minutes break for every 25 minutes of work)
  • Tracking: The timer counts up instead of down
